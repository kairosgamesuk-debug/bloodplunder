Blood & Plunder Historical Commanders (1700-1730)

🏴 Edward "Blackbeard" Teach (1680-1718)
Historical Command: Queen Anne's Revenge (1717-1718)
The most famous pirate of the Golden Age. Teach built his legend on psychology and terror—he famously braided slow-burning fuses into his beard during combat, creating a fearsome appearance. He was a skilled tactician who preferred negotiation and reputation to unnecessary violence; most targets surrendered at the sight of his flag. Hunted by Governor Spottswood of Virginia, he was killed in battle against pirate hunter Robert Maynard in 1718.
B&P Special Rules: Force gets +2 to attacker determination; can use false colors; grants Vendetta bonuses
⚔ John "Calico" Jack Rackham (1682-1718)
Historical Command: The Ranger (1718-1720)
A pragmatic captain known for accepting women as equals in his crew—Anne Bonny and Mary Read were full crew members, not disguises or stowaways. He designed the famous "Jack" flag: a black skull with two cutlasses crossed beneath it. Rackham emphasized tactics and cunning over brutality, capturing merchant vessels through superior seamanship rather than bloodshed. Captured and executed in Jamaica in 1720.
B&P Special Rules: Shallow Draft ships; Ruthless; all ships gain true Shallow Draft advantages
💀 Black Caesar (c.1700-1750)
Historical Background: Escaped slave who became one of the Caribbean's most feared pirate leaders
Born in Africa and enslaved, Caesar led a pirate confederation based in the Florida Keys. Unlike most pirate captains, Caesar led through democratic principles and loyalty rather than fear. His force was known for welcoming escaped slaves, creating a diverse crew united against slavery and oppression. He commanded shallow-draft vessels perfect for the Keys' waters.
B&P Special Rules: +4 to attacker determination; Ruthless; cannot use size 3+ ships (tactical choice); masters of ambush tactics
⚓ Anne Bonny & Mary Read (1697-1782 / 1685-1721)
Historical Record: Only documented female pirates of the Golden Age
Anne Bonny, daughter of an Irish attorney, disguised herself to join Calico Jack's crew. Mary Read also sailed disguised as a man. When authorities attacked their vessel, these two women were the only crew members to stand and fight—the male pirates fled below deck. Both were captured, convicted, and imprisoned. Bonny disappeared from historical record after her supposed pardon; Read died in prison of complications from illness. Their legacy represents resistance to gender norms and fearless combat ability.
B&P Connection: Flying Gang force represents a revolutionary crew structure where such differences were irrelevant to capability

They're freedom fighters in an age of tyranny, using the chaos of piracy to challenge established hierarchies.

🏴 Blood & Plunder: Unaligned Pirate Forces (1700-1730)

Core Philosophy: Pirates in Blood & Plunder operate outside national boundaries, serving no king or nation. They represent escaped slaves, bankrupt sailors, ideological rebels, and career criminals united by opportunity and necessity.

Available Forces:

  • Golden Age Pirates - General pirate force, flexible command options
  • Blackbeard's Men - +2 attacker; False Colors; Vendetta abilities
  • The Flying Gang - Shallow Draft; Ruthless; Card refresh mechanic
  • Caesar's Men - +4 attacker determination; Cannot use large ships; Amphibious superiority
  • The Last Buccaneers - Mixed multinational command options
  • Baymen - Defensive drunk rule; Elusive units; Size 3 ships maximum

Unit Composition: Core units typically include Pirates, Sea Dogs, and Marins. Support units draw from diverse sources: European Sailors, Jamaican Privateers, African Warriors, Later Flibustiers, Warrior Musketeers, and Pressed Men. This diversity reflects the multinational, multiracial nature of pirate crews.

⚓ Pirate Special Rules & Tactics

Ruthless: Pirate crews operate without mercy or convention. This rule reflects their willingness to use extreme tactics.

Shallow Draft Ships: Pirates favor small vessels like sloops and brigantines that can navigate coastal waters and rivers unavailable to naval forces. The Balandra exemplifies this advantage.

False Colors: Pirates can fly false flags to deceive enemies. At the start of each turn, roll d10: on 7+, the deception fails; otherwise, enemies cannot attack this turn unless the pirate force initiates combat.

Attacker Determination Bonuses: Different pirate forces gain +1 to +4 to their attack rolls in scenarios, reflecting their predatory nature and superior knowledge of hunting merchant vessels.

Force-Specific Mechanics:

  • Blackbeard's Men: Vendetta (extra damage vs. specific nationalities); +2 attacker bonus
  • Flying Gang: Can discard all Activation Cards once per game to refresh strategy
  • Caesar's Men: Maximum +4 attacker determination; exclusive amphibious advantages

🚢 The Sloop Balandra - Pirate Perfection

Ship Type: Small, fast, maneuverable sailing vessel (30-50 tons)

Historical Examples:

  • Queen Anne's Revenge (Blackbeard's flagship 1717-1718)
  • The Ranger (Calico Jack Rackham's command 1718-1720)
  • Various unnamed sloops used by Caesar and other pirate confederations

Tactical Advantages:

  • Speed: Can rapidly approach or disengage from combat
  • Shallow Draft: Navigates rivers, bays, and coastal waters where military ships cannot follow
  • Crew Capacity: 100-150 persons; high crew-to-tonnage ratio for boarding operations
  • Cargo Flexibility: Can carry plunder from multiple vessels simultaneously
  • Minimal Armament: 4-8 cannons; relies on speed and boarding rather than broadside artillery

Role in Ocean's Teams: In 200-point forces, the Balandra serves as command platform, mobile fire support, and deployment base. Commanders often remain aboard vessel during combat, using deck crew and boarding parties for tactical control.

🎭 Pirate Crew Composition & Diversity

Why Piracy Attracted Diverse Recruits:

  • Escaped Slaves: African Warriors and later military personnel. Pirates offered freedom and share in plunder
  • Pressed Sailors: Forced into naval service, many deserted to pirate crews offering democratic vote
  • Runaway Soldiers: Deserters from failed military operations found acceptance among pirates
  • Indigenous Peoples: Some pirate crews included Native Americans operating in their own waters
  • Economic Migrants: Landless Europeans seeking fortune without nation-state oversight

This diversity is reflected in Blood & Plunder unit availability: Pirates can include European Sailors, African Warriors, Jamaican Privateers (mixed race crews), Native American warriors, and mercenary specialists. This represents the truly multicultural nature of historical pirate operations.
