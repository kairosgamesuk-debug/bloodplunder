Week 1 — Sortie from Castillo San Marcos

LandOfficial 100 pts/side2 players6 turns St. Augustine, 11 Nov 1702

During the 1702 English siege of St. Augustine, the Spanish sortied from Castillo San Marcos to burn the houses nearest the fort so the English could not use them as firing positions. They succeeded in burning several, later reimbursing owners between 50 and 1,200 pesos.

Table4'×3' land, minimal natural terrain. The English (Defender) edge represents the edge of St. Augustine: place 5–8 buildings of one or two sections in two rough rows — one along the board's middle, one close to the Defender's edge.
DeploymentDefender (English): half their units (round down) within 6" of their board edge; the rest arrive via the Reserves rule (rulebook p.146). Attacker (Spanish): within 4" of their board edge.
Special
  • Attacking units may add Torches equipment at no cost.
  • If Wind Speed is ever positive, apply a −1 bonus to all Set Fire actions per +1 Wind Speed.
Objectives
  • Defender gains a Strike Point for each Destroyed Building.
  • Defender gains a Strike Point for each building still burning at end of turn 6.
  • Attacker gains a Strike Point if no building is burning or destroyed at end of turn 4 or later.
  • Attacker gains a Strike Point if no building is destroyed at end of turn 6.
Open play100 pts or less. Attacker may not include Field Guns or Cavalry. Deliberately scaled to one English + one Spanish nationality box.

Week 2 — Hornigold and Teach Attack La Concorde

SeaOfficial 227 v 207 pts2–3 players6 turns St. Vincent, 28 Nov 1717

In late November 1717 Hornigold and Blackbeard chased the 250-ton French ship La Concorde, whose captain Pierre Dosset had 70% of his crew down with "scurvy or the bloody flux" and could not man a full complement of guns. She surrendered after two volleys and became Teach's flagship — renamed Queen Anne's Revenge.

Table4'×4' or 4'×6' sea board, no extra terrain.
DeploymentDefender: part of their ship within 4" of their board corner, oriented per the diagram. No Defending units may start assigned to any artillery. Attacker(s): anywhere outside 24" from the Defender's ship.
Special
  • Before the game the Defender must take 2 Lucky Hits to their Rigging (storm damage).
  • The board may not be scrolled. A ship sailing off any edge but the Escape Edge hits a rocky shoal and is grounded.
  • With 3 players, swap the Teach Character for the Commander version and let the French player add ~25 points. The two pirates work as a team.
Victory
  • Defender wins immediately if they sail off the Escape edge.
  • Defender wins if there are no Attacking units aboard at the end of round 6.
  • Attackers win immediately if they capture the Defender's ship.
Open playAttacker force best split into two smaller ships, and may be 10% larger than the Defender if so split. Defender should be in one larger ship.

Week 3 — Prisoner Rescue at Rio de Janeiro

LandOfficial 200 v 152 pts2 players6 turns Rio de Janeiro, Sep 1711

The first French attack on Rio (1710) under Jean-François Du Clerc was a disaster — 600 of 1,500 men captured. René Duguay-Trouin seized the chance for glory and plunder, sailing straight into the harbour on a misty morning against poorly organised Portuguese defences. The city fell, the prisoners were freed, and the French escaped with nearly 4 million pounds of plunder.

Table3'×4' land mat, at least 4 buildings representing a port city. More may be used, but 4 must be designated searchable and numbered 1–4. Each searchable building must have an access point on the board centreline, sit at least 6" from any board edge and at least 4" from each other.
DeploymentDefender: anywhere 9" away from the Attacker's zone. One in every three Defender units is held off-table as Reserves (p.146) — the Defender picks which. Non-Reserve units set up within 3" of the indicated edge; Reserves enter from any table edge in the Defender's zone. Activation Cards are drawn for off-board units at the start of the turn they enter. Attacker: within 4" of their board edge.
Special
  • After setup but before placing forces, the Defender secretly writes down which building holds the prisoners.
  • When an Attacking unit enters that building, the Defender adds a "Captured Enemy Soldier" to a unit inside. That is the objective for the rest of the game.
  • The prisoners may not be chosen as a casualty in melee or shooting.
  • While holding the prisoners, reduce movement by 1".
  • Prisoners may be transferred to a friendly unit within 2" with a standard action.
  • Captured Enemy Soldier (Hostage/Advisor) — no Special Rules. Extra ability: each time your opponent draws a new hand, you may look at one random card per 3 cards drawn (min 1).
Objectives
  • Either side gains a Strike Point if the Prisoners are controlled by the enemy.
  • At end of turn 4+, the Attacker gains a Strike Point if the Prisoners are not yet revealed.
  • Attacker automatically loses if they don't control the Prisoners by the end of turn 6.
  • Defender automatically loses if the Prisoners are moved off the Attacker's board edge.
Open playAttacker may not include Cavalry or Artillery.

Objectives (scored at end of game)

Week 6 — Hatfield Raid, 1724

LandOfficial 84 v 81 pts2 players6 turns Hatfield MA, 18 Jun 1724

The Wabanaki were excluded from the 1713 treaties ending Queen Anne's War, and English expansion past the Kennebec led to Dummer's War (1723–25). In the western theatre — Gray Lock's War — Chief Gray Lock raided from his Missisquoi base at the north end of Lake Champlain; in his second summer campaign he fell upon men working the fields outside Hatfield.

Table3'×4' with a house in the centre, fields around the house, and woodland area terrain around the edges, as shown.
DeploymentDefender: in the fields within 4" of the long centreline; may not deploy within buildings or within 5" of the building. Attacker: within 4" of the long board edges. The Wabanaki may also use Lay In Wait during setup and Vanguard after setup.
Special

  • Uses the No Quarter scenario rule.
  • Fields count as Rough Terrain and provide no Cover.
Objectives
  • At end of turn 6 the Attacker gains 1 Strike Point per Defending unit left on the board.
  • The Attacker gains 2 Strike Points if their own Commander is removed as a casualty.

Lieutenant Robert Maynard spied Blackbeard's balandra and ran both his sloops aground while closing. Teach chose to board Maynard's sloop; Maynard's men, hidden below decks, erupted into a fierce melee. Teach fell with 5 shot wounds and 20 cuts, and an unknown Scotsman severed his head.

Victory

  • Defender wins automatically if their ship moves off the Attacker's table edge.

Week 9 — Charles Vane's Escape from Nassau

SeaOfficial ~650 pts/side6 players6 turns Nassau, 22 Jul 1718

One team plays Charles Vane and his accomplices attempting to escape the blockaded harbour at Nassau; the other plays Woodes Rogers, come to restore law and order to New Providence. Historically there was little fighting — the fireship forced the British warships to cut their anchor cables, letting Vane slip out the far end of the bay next morning. This scenario lets them fight it out.

Table4'×6' sea. All board edges but the Defender's are Impassable, and within 2" of those three edges is Shoal terrain.
Army ScaleAs Week 5 — 3 players per side, only the General draws activation cards (equal to the largest friendly company's unit count); each card played activates one unit per player in that army. Each General starts with 9 Fortune Points.
DeploymentDefenders (British): ships Anchored, exactly 24" from the Defender's board edge in a blockade as shown. All Defending units must deploy Unassigned.
Attackers (Pirates): the Brigantine Fire Ship anywhere in the Attacker's half, no closer than 24" to the Defender's ships, sail setting 3", with the Longboat in tow. Remaining ships within 4" of the Attacker's edge at the lowest sail setting above 0".
Special
  • Limited Visibility: Night — ships visible from 24"; the burning ship and anything within 6" of the fire is visible from anywhere on the board.
  • Attacking ships may sail off the Defender's board edge.
  • Commanders do not make Strike Tests, but Strike Points are tallied to decide the winner at the end of round 6 (or earlier if time runs short).
Fireship
  • The Pirate Brigantine starts with a Fire Critical Hit marker on one section; this marker may never be removed.
  • Before a British unit activates, if it is within 8" of the Fireship it must take a Fatigue test with one d10.
  • When a burning section collides with another ship: apply a Fire Critical Hit to the hull section touched, apply +4 to the die on the collision table (p.88), and all enemy units on the struck ship take a Fatigue test with 1d10.
Objectives
  • Defenders gain 1 Strike Point per Fire Critical Hit marker on their ships.
  • Defenders gain 1 Strike Point per destroyed deck of their ships.
  • Defenders gain 1 Strike Point per Attacker ship (size 2+) that sails off the Defenders' edge.
  • Attackers gain 1 Strike Point per friendly unit removed as casualties.
  • The Attacker takes no Strike Points for anything related to the Fireship.
Open playAttackers: P1 (General) ~250 pts including a fireship with skeleton crew, no size 4 ships; two more players at 200 pts in 1–3 deck ships. Defenders: P1 ~150 pts with no ship, starting aboard P2's ship; P2 300 pts on a size 4 ship with plenty of cannons (leaving room for P1's units); P3 200 pts on a size 2–3 ship.

Scenario I — Men of Fortune

LandOfficial 100–200 pts2 players6 turns

Not content to accept the hand dealt them, bold buccaneers and pirates often took desperate measures to make their own fortune, attempting to change or escape the decrees of Lady Fortune.

Table3'×4' or 3'×3' land with plenty of natural area terrain. Place 4 Fortune Token coins along the centreline, equal distances from each player's edge — about 9" apart on a 4'-wide board, 7" apart on a 3'-wide board. Suggested: premium fortune coins nearer the centre, smaller coins to the sides.
DeploymentStarting with the Defender, players deploy within 4" of their board edges.
Special
  • Players begin with 2 fewer Fortune Tokens than usual.
  • At the start of each turn a player may claim up to 1 Fortune Token from the table if they started the round with at least one unit in base contact with it AND no enemy unit started the round within 4" of it.
Objective
  • A player gains 1 Strike Point if they have no unspent Fortune Tokens in their supply at the end of a round.

Scenario II — The Fickle Sea

SeaOfficial 150–250 pts/player2–6 players6 turns

Sea rovers spent years mastering the ways of the sea, yet it remained unpredictable and uncontrollable. Shifting winds keep crews on high alert as conditions change rapidly. Review the Ship Advanced Maneuvers rules before starting.

Table4'×4' for 2–4 players or 4'×6' for 4–6. Whole board is Deep Water; extra water terrain at players' discretion. Wind Speed starts at +1.
Pre-gameEach player may either add a Ship's Wheel upgrade to one ship or add the Sailing Master Character as an extra model to a unit (prefer a unit without a Character; this may break the 1-Character-per-50pts limit). If neither is legal for a force, that force's Commander gains Tough.
DeploymentStarting with a Defender and alternating with Attackers, each player deploys their entire force with part of their ship(s) within 6" of their board edge. All ships must deploy more than 24" from opposing ships.
Special
  • With 4 or 6 players, split into Attacker/Defender teams by Attacker rolls before the game. Use standard activation or a Multiplayer Variant from No Peace Beyond the Line.
  • At the start of each round, before drawing activation cards, roll 1d10 on the Wind Table and apply immediately. Teams alternate who rolls, starting with the Defenders.
  • After a size 2+ ship moves, any unit assigned to the Sweeps takes a Fatigue Test with a single d10.
  • While Wind Speed is +1 or more, apply a +1 penalty to all Grapple Tests.
Objectives
  • A player with at least one Critical Hit marker on their Rigging at the end of a round gains 1 Strike Point.
  • With more than 2 players, use the No Quarter rule and ignore Strike Tests — play until one fleet strikes its colours or turn 6 ends.
Forces150–250 pts per player. In 4- or 6-player games sizes may vary so long as each team totals the same (e.g. three 150-pt forces vs two 225-pt forces = 450 each). Assign a unit to Sheets & Braces or keep one unassigned to react to wind changes.
Sailing Master (Character)Unit restriction: only units with Sailors or Expert Sailors. Command Range 4". Command Points 1 — may only grant a Change Sail Setting or Advanced Maneuver extra action. Special Rules: Sailing Master, Expert Sailors. Ship's Wheel (Upgrade)Size 3+ ship only. Apply a −1 bonus to Sailing Tests when attempting Advanced Maneuvers.

Scenario III — Escape by Sea

AmphibiousOfficial 100–200 pts2 players6 turns

A band of buccaneers has landed and seized an important prisoner, and is now attempting to escape by sea with the enemy hot on their heels.

Table4'×4', 50% land / 50% water. Add a dock near the board centre, plus one boat and one size 2+ ship, both at Sail Setting 0" and grappled to the dock. The ship may face any direction except towards land. The wind starts blowing from the land towards the sea.
Pre-gameAfter rolling for Attacker/Defender: the Attacker adds the Musician model to their Commander's unit; the Defender attaches the Hostage Character to their Commander's unit. This may break the 1-Character-per-50pts limit.
DeploymentDefender: within 10" of their board corner as shown; they may deploy their smallest unit on the ship at board centre — this may not be the Commander's unit. Attacker: within 8" of their corner as shown.
Special
  • Reduce any ship movement by 1" if the Boat is grappled to the Ship.
  • The Hostage may not be ransomed until the 6th turn.
Objectives
  • If the Defender moves the Hostage off the full sea board edge, they immediately win.
  • If the Attacker controls the Hostage at the end of any turn, the Defender gains 1 Strike Point.
  • The Defender gains 1 Strike Point if the Attacker controls the ship.
ForcesLand forces of 100–200 pts each. No ships or boats beyond those in the setup (which count as part of neither force).
Hostage (Hostage/Advisor)Special Rules: High Standing. Extra: a unit making a Ranged attack against the unit containing this model must first take a Fatigue test with a single d10, and may not attempt the attack at all if it already has 2 Fatigue. The opposing Commander may spend a free action and give one Fortune Token to the controlling player to remove the Hostage. This Ransom requires both the Hostage and the opposing Commander to be out of melee, within 12", and on the same landmass or ship (or on grappled ships). Extra: at the start of the battle the opposing force is affected by Terror, unless it also has a Musician.

Quake 1692

LandPort Royal — different game 30 Pieces of Eight6 turns
Note: a Port Royal scenario, not Blood & Plunder. Included because it ships free in the B&P kit and the event table is worth stealing for your own games. By Erich of the Thieves' Harbor blog.

The 1692 Jamaica earthquake destroyed the western portion of Port Royal; roughly 2,000 died in the quake and another 3,000 afterward from injuries, disease and rival crews. It came in three escalating parts, with liquefied sand and waves that lifted ships above the buildings. Ideal as the opening game of a new campaign.

TableEach player prepares a standard 30 Pieces of Eight list. Board 2'×2' (larger with more than 2 players), a dense, lively cityscape.
DeploymentStandard deployment is not used. Each player deploys their Captain/Leader within 4" of opposite board edges (North and South). Remaining crew are scattered 1d10" from the board centre — roll a d10, the triangle's top gives direction, the number gives distance in inches. Models may land in buildings, on terrain, etc. In each board quarter, scatter a Plunder Token 1d5" from that quarter's centre (4 total).
Special
  • 6 turns, but crews cannot withdraw and do not roll Strike Tests — they must survive.
  • Use the Quake 1692 Events Table instead of the standard one. At the end of Rounds 1, 3 and 5, roll once, twice and three times respectively.
  • Only the Captain/Leader begins active. Other crew are Inactive until a friendly model interacts with them within 1". Opponents cannot attack or interact with Inactive crew.
Objective
  • Locate your scattered crew while grabbing Bonus Plunder, which aids the games following the quake.
Post-gameCharacters killed through Event Table tragedies suffer a −2/−20 penalty for Crew/Officers when rolling injuries. Rescued characters gain +1 experience; if the entire crew survives the Captain gains +2. When dividing shares, pay Captain and Officers first, then Crew as best you can, and skip the Mutiny step even if some of the company went unpaid.
d10Event
1Tidal Wave — a wave crashes onto a random east/west edge. All models within 8" take an Experience Test; on failure they are pushed 4" away from the edge. Crashing into terrain causes a single Fatigue test. The region floods and is rough terrain until end of turn.
2Aftershocks — all models take an Experience Test; on failure, knocked prone.
3Distraction — a random active member of each crew makes a Resolve Test.
4Falling Debris — a random Character among all forces suffers a 2-success Ranged Attack with a Damage bonus of −1.
5Shifting Fortunes — scatter a corpse 6" from a random character. Looting it with an Interact Action gains a ring worth 1d5 Pieces of Eight.
6Panic — scatter 5 Civilians 1d10" from board centre. They block line of sight and movement and automatically fail all tests. If killed, replace with a lootable corpse. Killing a Civilian gives that player −2 Fortune Points next turn, and this stacks.
7A Brief Respite — all active characters may remove a point of fatigue.
8Misplaced Munitions — scatter a Plunder Token 1d10" from table centre. Looting it grants a peculiar equipment (Core rulebook p.77).
9Debris Cloud — for the next turn treat all range bands as one further for ranged attacks; melee at +1 penalty.
10Liquefaction — a random building sinks. Roll d10: on 5+ the building and all models and tokens are lost beneath the sands and removed. Otherwise the terrain is destroyed and becomes rough terrain. All models within suffer a 4-success Melee attack with a Damage bonus of −1.

Beached Ship (+ "Shipwrecked Prize" variant)

AmphibiousOfficial — Raise the Black 6 turns

The Ghost Ship — 2022 Halloween special

SeaFan-made 100 pts solo / 50 per player co-op1+ players, co-op
Partial entry. The full rules exist only as a flattened image and a Google Drive PDF — neither yields extractable text. The table size, deployment distances, turn count and the Ghost Ship movement/Fatigue tables are not reproducible here. Grab the source below.

In the 17th-century Caribbean, ghost ships were common but mundane — vessels abandoned because of violence, disease, weather or bad luck. Buccaneer crews made them regularly when merging crews or abandoning a sloop for a captured prize. Found ships were reclaimed and salvaged, common enough that every colonial nation had laws and courts to establish a claim was Salvage and not Piracy. This Ghost Ship is not supernatural in origin — it is simply a ship without a crew, found in heavy fog, sitting low in the water, sails in disarray, decks void of life.

SetupYou need a ship to serve as the Ghost Ship — a Brigantine or Light Frigate is recommended, but any will do. There is no opposing player force.
Core mechanicThe Ghost Ship's movements are determined by a card drawn from your deck at the start of the first, second and last activation of a turn. In co-op play, alternate which player's deck the Ghost Ship draws from.
ObjectiveBoard and claim the Ghost Ship as salvage before your crew loses its nerve. The opposition is Fatigue and Resolve pressure, not casualties.
List buildingGood Resolve, plenty of Rally actions and rules like Tough that manage Fatigue are very helpful. Muskets, Pistols, Swivels and even Cannons are almost useless — though you can try to slow the Ghost Ship by targeting its rigging. Large units don't help; small units are fine. You will burn through Fortune Points. Co-op options: 50 pts per player sharing a ship, or a 150-pt force with one player running the commander's unit and the other running everything else. Plays in under an hour.
By Guy Rheuark, Blood & Pigment. Article · Scenario PDF · Scenario sheet (image). Sample lists: French Buccaneers · Native American · English Royal Navy. The author notes it can be modified into a competitive two-player fight over the Ghost Ship.

Official1 player works with any scenario

A free rules module letting one player play against an AI-controlled Opposing Force (OPFOR). Any rule not specifically altered works as normal, and Firelock notes you should exercise common sense to help the AI along.

Movement
  • Aggressive: move directly toward the nearest enemy; end in cover if available, but priority is ending as close to the enemy as possible.
  • Tactical: move toward the nearest objective not under this force's control, then toward the closest enemies, taking maximum advantage of cover.
  • Retreat: move out of Line of Sight of as many enemies as possible, prioritising the closest; seek the best cover, priority being an objective and then the home board edge.
AttacksWhen charging, target the closest unit. For Shoot actions, choose first units in the open with a target number under 9, then the unit with the lowest modified target number; always prioritise units that have not yet activated; ties random. OPFOR units always make defensive attacks if possible.
Exceptions
  • Gun Crews: units on artillery never take move actions, except to move field-carriage guns into line of sight.
  • Guerillas (Hidden, Elusive, Skirmishers, Cunning and/or Quick): use those rules as often as possible; only ever move within 8" of a unit that has Fatigue.
  • Shooting Specialists (Drilled, Expertly Drilled or Marksmen) with 2+ actions: use those actions only if they reduce the target number to 8 or less.
  • Aggressive Units — not armed with Muskets/Carbines/Bows, not crewing artillery, and armed with explosives (even if also carrying firearms) — always prioritise moving and charging before shooting.
  • Bayonets: units with Bayonets and no loaded shooting weapons plug bayonets within 8" of opposing units, and while fixed count as Aggressive units.
  • Cavalry: mounted units always attempt to stay out of LoS, but priority is still to close on their target.
